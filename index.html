Rules and Regulations
  • Top 10 teams from Round 1 will qualify for Round 2, which will be an offline event on 15th February.
  • Each team will be given an initial buffer of two easy questions.
  • Once the buffer questions are solved, one of the team members can throw darts to determine the difficulty of the next question they will receive.
  • All questions are worth the same amount of points.
  • The dart board will have three concentric rings; the outermost representing difficult questions, the center ring representing medium questions and the innermost representing easy questions.
  • Balloons will be attached on top of each ring. The team member throwing the darts must burst a balloon in order to determine the question difficulty.
  • Each team will be given three darts per question selection. If the team member throwing the darts is unable to burst a balloon in these three chances, the team will be given a difficult question to solve (Note that this is not best of 3. For example, if a balloon is burst in the second chance, a third chance will not be given).
  • A team will only be given one question at a time to solve (except for the initial buffer). In case a team is unable to solve a question given to them, they are allowed to forfeit the question and go for dart throwing again (this is allowed for the initial buffer as well).
  • A team must attempt to solve each question given to them for a minimum time period of 10 minutes, before which forfeit will not be allowed. Forfeit will also incur a penalty as per the question difficulty.
  • A team will be given a new question only if they either solve the current question given to them or forfeit it.
